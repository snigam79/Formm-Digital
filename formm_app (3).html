What is Formm Score?
Your Formm Score is a 0–100 rating of your movement quality across all analysed exercises. It's calculated from five dimensions — form accuracy, range of motion, stability, symmetry, and improvement rate — benchmarked against peer-reviewed sports science research. The more exercises you analyse, the more accurate it becomes. Purple = Formm Score only. It never appears elsewhere in the app.

Training Load (ACWR)
Acute:chronic workload ratio
What is ACWR?
ACWR (Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio) compares how hard you've trained this week against your 28-day rolling average. A ratio of 1.0 means you're training at your usual level. The sweet spot is 0.8–1.3. Above 1.5, injury risk rises significantly. Below 0.8, you risk detraining. Every session you log with an RPE rating updates this number automatically.

Session RPE
Rate perceived exertion · log session load
What is RPE?
RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) is a 1–10 scale for how hard a session felt. Combined with duration, it gives your session load in AU (Arbitrary Units): AU = RPE × minutes. A 7/10 effort for 60 minutes = 420 AU. This feeds directly into your ACWR and Readiness score. You should log RPE within 30 minutes of finishing — that's when your perception is most accurate.

Wearables
Raw signals · no proprietary scores
How wearables work in Formm
Formm reads raw physiological signals (HRV, RHR, sleep stages, respiratory rate) from your wearable. It does not use proprietary scores like WHOOP Recovery or Oura Readiness — those are black boxes. Formm builds its own interpretation using the same signals, calibrated to your personal baseline. The longer you use it, the more accurate your readiness score becomes.
