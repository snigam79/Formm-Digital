05 · Set Score Calculation
How the
score is built
The set form score is not a simple average of rep scores. It is a weighted composite that penalises late-set degradation more than early-set degradation, and weights fault severity differently by exercise.
Scoring weights — squat

Why late reps are weighted more heavily
A form breakdown at rep 7 of 8 tells you more about the athlete's limit than a form breakdown at rep 2 of 8. The scoring engine weights the final 30% of the set at 1.4× the weight of the opening reps.

06 · The Data Model
Where every
number lives
The rep-by-rep model depends on three database tables. The rep_data table is the most critical — it cannot be backfilled and every longitudinal feature depends on it existing from session one.
rep_data
Never delete · Cannot be backfilled
One row per rep per analysis session. The atomic unit of everything Formm does longitudinally. Every feature — fatigue curve, session comparison, validation study, form-load interaction — depends on this table existing.
id, user_id, analysis_session_id
rep_number, knee_flexion_max, knee_valgus_left, knee_valgus_right
hip_flexion_max, trunk_lean, lumbar_deviation, symmetry_pct
form_score_this_rep, captured_at
analysis_sessions
Aggregated from rep_data · Updated each session
One row per filming session. Stores per-session averages so Page 2 loads quickly without recalculating from rep_data every time. Session score, top fault, per-metric averages.
id, user_id, exercise, session_date
session_score, reps_analysed, camera_angle
avg_valgus_l, avg_valgus_r, avg_depth, avg_symmetry
top_fault, improvement_vs_last, created_at
sessions
RPE logs · Powers ACWR
One row per RPE log. The load management data. Combined with analysis_sessions on the same date to produce the form-load interaction insight.
id, user_id, session_date, exercise
rpe, duration_minutes, session_load_au
is_confirmed, created_at
The form-load interaction — Formm's unique insight
When rep_data and sessions are joined by user and date, Formm can detect: your squat form score drops 14 points when your ACWR is above 1.3.
This insight — your technique deteriorates under high training load, specifically — is completely invisible without both data streams. No other platform produces it. It requires the rep_data table and the sessions table to exist simultaneously and be joined at the user level.
