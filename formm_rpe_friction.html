Friction 01
The problem
The result screen feels like the destination. Users see their form score and leave. The RPE screen is a separate navigation they never reach. High form-analysis engagement — zero load data.
The solution
The session does not end until both steps are complete. The progress bar shows "Step 1 of 2 — Step 2 of 2." The "Record this session" button is the exit from the result screen — not a separate journey. Same flow, same session.
Progress indicator — "Step 1 of 2 complete" on result screen makes the RPE log feel like a required second step, not optional extra.
2 taps maximum — tap RPE number, tap Log. Duration is pre-filled from the camera timer. Under 8 seconds total.
Language change — "Record this session" not "Log RPE." The user is doing something for themselves, not filling in a form.
Target: ≥ 70% of sessions logged within 10 min

Friction 02
The problem
Cost of logging is now. Reward — the readiness brief — arrives tomorrow morning. Without an immediate visible payoff at the moment of tapping, the habit does not form.
The solution
Two things happen the instant an RPE number is tapped: the ACWR bar animates to the new position and the next session prescription appears. Value is tangible within 3 seconds — before the user even taps Log.
Live ACWR update — tap RPE 7 and watch the bar move, the number update, the colour shift. The model is running in real time in front of them.
Immediate prescription — "Next session: RPE 6–7 in 48h" appears before they tap Log. A concrete output that did not exist 2 seconds ago.
Confirmed notification — "Recovery brief set for 7am tomorrow" makes the deferred reward feel real and scheduled.
Target: next session prescription shown after ≥ 80% of logs

Friction 03
The problem
RPE is subjective. A beginner's 7 and an advanced athlete's 7 are different physiological states. Inconsistent logs → noisy ACWR → unreliable readiness briefs → user stops trusting the product.
The solution
Anchored descriptions on every RPE button, permanently visible. After 6 sessions: personal calibration card shows the user their own scale in AU. After 20 sessions: RPE drift detection enabled.
Anchors always visible — "RPE 8 = speaking hard, rest tomorrow" beneath every button. Tap the overlay button to see the full-screen explainer (Friction 10 solution).
Personal calibration card — after 6 sessions, tap "Log session" to see what their data shows. Tells them their scale is working.
Target: RPE variance < 1.5 SD within same exercise

Friction 04
The problem
Did the session take 55 or 75 minutes? Users genuinely do not know. ±20 minute error at RPE 7 = ±140 AU load variance. That shifts ACWR from 1.2 (safe) to 1.4 (elevated) — producing false alerts that erode trust.
The solution
Three layers: (1) Camera timer auto-detects duration for filmed sessions. (2) History-based quick-tap chips for non-filmed sessions. (3) Never a blank text field — always a pre-filled starting point.
Auto-detected from camera — the phone knows when the analysis started and ended. Pre-filled with one-tap edit option.
History chips — the user's 4 most common session durations shown as one-tap options. 60 min pre-selected as the most recent. Correct in most cases with zero effort.
Target: duration pre-filled in ≥ 85% of log attempts

Friction 05
The problem
Form analysis and RPE log are two disconnected features. A user who films a squat and leaves has engaged fully with one — and generated zero load data. High form-analysis users and zero ACWR accuracy.
The solution
The session progress bar makes both steps explicit and sequential. "1 of 2" on the result screen. "2 of 2" on the RPE screen. The session is not complete until both bars are filled. The completion button says "Complete session" — not "Log RPE."
Target: % of form analyses followed by RPE log within 10 min

Friction 06
The problem
455 AU means nothing. ACWR 1.3 means nothing. Users cannot decode these numbers, so they do not value the logging that produces them, and do not change behaviour based on the output.
The solution
Design rule: every number has a verdict. The verdict is the product. The number is context. The bar shows three zones (under / optimal / over). Tapping a number shows a plain-English verdict before anything else.
Tap RPE 4 → "Light session. Your ACWR is low — you can push harder."
Tap RPE 7 → "Solid session. ACWR is in the optimal zone."
Tap RPE 9 → "Very hard. ACWR is elevated — rest before next session."
Target: user taps through to ACWR detail ≥ 40% of sessions

Friction 07
The problem
One missed log = 7 days of inaccurate ACWR. Two missed sessions per week = permanently noisy model. Unreliable readiness briefs → user stops trusting the product → stops logging entirely.
The solution
Three mechanisms: (1) Re-engagement notification fires 20h after missed session. (2) ACWR shows as "estimated" with amber dot instead of confirmed teal — visible difference. (3) Retroactive logging allowed up to 48h.
Estimated vs confirmed ACWR — amber dot + "~1.2 estimated" vs teal dot + "1.3 confirmed." The gap between them is visible and motivating.
Specific re-engagement message — "Yesterday's squat session isn't logged — your ACWR could be higher." References their actual data, not generic guilt.
Target: < 2 missed logs per user per week at day 30

Friction 08
The problem
The ideal readiness brief uses HRV + sleep + ACWR. Most alpha users have no wearable. A brief that never fully works for 80% of users erodes perceived value before the habit forms.
The solution
Launch with a load-based brief that is genuinely useful without a wearable. ACWR alone is meaningful. Label it "Load-based readiness" vs "Full readiness." Health Connect provides sleep data free on all Android phones — no wearable needed.
Load-based brief at launch — ACWR + training history only. Honest labelling. Genuinely useful even without HRV.
Health Connect sleep — free on Android, no hardware needed. Adds sleep quality to the brief immediately.
Non-pushy upsell — "Connect for full brief" shown only after 7 days of regular logging. Not at onboarding.
Target: ≥ 60% of users seeing readiness brief daily by day 30

Friction 09
The problem
As users get fitter, the same workout feels easier. RPE drops from 7 to 6 without load increasing. ACWR appears stable while actual load drops. The model becomes less accurate without the user realising — or being told.
The solution
Every 8 weeks, detect if average RPE has dropped more than 1.0 point without a corresponding load increase. Show a card that reframes this as good news (you are fitter) and asks if they are increasing load to match. Doubles as a training progression prompt.
Drift is reframed as progress — "Your sessions feel easier — that is progress." Not an accusation. A celebration that includes a prompt.
Two options shown — "I've increased load" (confirms calibration is still valid) or "Show me how" (training progression guidance). Both paths serve the user.
Target: RPE drift flagged in ≥ 80% of qualifying users at 8 weeks

Friction 10
The problem
Most gym-goers have never used RPE. A blank 10-number scale with no context is disorienting. First-session drop-off is highest when the RPE screen appears for the first time with no explanation.
The solution
A 90-second full-screen explainer that appears once — on the first RPE screen, in context, after a real session. Three anchor examples. Dismisses with one tap. Never shown again. No onboarding deck. No tutorial video.
Contextual — not pre-emptive — shown after the user has just finished a session and needs to rate it. The context makes the explanation immediately relevant.
Three anchors only — 5, 7, 9. Not all 10. Enough to calibrate. Simple enough to remember.
Confirmed it will not repeat — "This screen will not appear again" removes the anxiety of onboarding fatigue.
Target: first-session RPE log completion ≥ 80%
