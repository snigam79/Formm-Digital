World-building · Foundation

The Formm World — what people are buying entry to

Before a single ad runs, Formm needs a world that people want to belong to. This world has a worldview, a villain, a belief, and a tribe identity.

"Build a world. Invite people in. Let them decode the product. The launch is a formality."
@austinsutor — The New Playbook
🌍 The Worldview
"Every serious athlete has always had access to sports science. You just weren't one of them. Until now."
The fitness industry sells harder workouts. Formm sells smarter ones. The world Formm builds is one where the gym-goer in Gurgaon has the same movement intelligence as an IPL cricketer. That's the belief system. Everything — the content, the community, the product — should reinforce it.
🦹 The Villain
"Blind grinding. Training hard without training smart."
Every strong brand has a villain. Formm's villain isn't a competitor — it's the idea that more reps, more hours, more pain equals more progress. The villain is the gym culture that celebrates effort over quality. Formm is the antidote: precision over punishment.
🪙 The Tribe Identity
"The Formm athlete trains with intention. They know their numbers. They move with quality."
People in the Formm world call themselves athletes — not gym-goers. They track form quality, not just weight lifted. They talk about ACWR and readiness scores. Using Formm is how they signal they belong to this tribe. The Scout Score is the badge of membership.
🔮 The Mystery
"What's your Scout Score?" — a question that creates curiosity before it creates a download.
The post says "mystery is the modern marketing budget." Formm's mystery is the Scout Score. Don't explain it upfront. Let people see others with Scout Scores and wonder what theirs is. The number is meaningless without context — but it creates desire to find out. That curiosity is the acquisition funnel.
The Formm Manifesto (3 lines — put this everywhere)
"Your coach has a sports science team.
You've been training without one.
That changes now."
Use as Instagram bio, app store description opener, onboarding screen, and the hook for every piece of content. This is the world in three sentences.
Pillar 1 — Story

Story: The worldview Formm builds before it builds anything else

Story is the operating system of the brand. It answers: what do we believe, who do we believe it for, and what would the world look like if we were right?

🎯
The Positioning Statement — say this, not that
The exact language Formm uses to describe itself. Consistency here creates the world. Inconsistency dilutes it.
STORY FOUNDATIONUSE EVERYWHERE
Don't say this
"AI fitness app" · "workout tracker" · "form correction" · "gym tool" · "exercise analyser" · "fitness app with AI"
Say this
"Your personal sports science team" · "Movement intelligence" · "Train with intent, not just effort" · "The sports science platform for serious athletes"
🏆
The Scout Score as cultural currency
The Scout Score is Formm's single most powerful world-building tool. It needs to become something people talk about, compare, and aspire to — before they even understand what it means.
BRAND IDENTITYACQUISITION ENGINE
1
Never explain the Scout Score unprompted. Let people see it in content, wonder what it is, and have to ask or download to find out. Mystery is the point. "@username just hit Scout Score 74 — what's yours?" as a post caption generates curiosity-driven downloads.
2
Create milestone culture. When users hit a new Scout Score level (Rookie → Athlete → Elite), give them a shareable card. The share is organic reach and it signals tribe membership. "Just hit Athlete level on Formm 🏆" is the fitness equivalent of a Spotify Wrapped.
3
Leak the language into the culture. Partner with gym coaches in NCR to start using "Scout Score" as vocabulary in their own content, even without mentioning Formm explicitly. When people Google "Scout Score fitness", Formm should be the only answer.
4
Compare to accessible aspirational figures. "An IPL cricketer's movement quality score is typically 85+. The average recreational gym-goer is 58. What's yours?" This frames the Scout Score as something elite athletes use, not a gamification mechanic.
📣
The Founding Story — Samrat's personal narrative
The post shows @davidprotein built $140M by acting like a fashion house, not a protein brand. Formm's founder story needs to be as much a part of the brand as the product.
FOUNDER-LEDHIGH LEVERAGE
1
Build the founder's voice on LinkedIn + Instagram now, not after the app launches. Post weekly about the science behind what Formm is building. "We analysed 500 squat videos and found that 73% of gym-goers in Delhi have the same knee collapse pattern — here's why and what to do." This is content that builds the world before the product is in their hands.
2
The origin story framing: "I watched elite athletes get sports science that the average gym-goer could never access. The gap wasn't talent. It was information. So I built Formm." This is the founding narrative — specific, personal, and directly connected to the worldview.
3
Document the build publicly. Share the behind-the-scenes of building the AI — the data collection, the accuracy benchmarks, the first real user getting a form check result. The people who follow the build become the launch audience. They don't need to be sold to. They already believe in it.

Signal: Content that makes people feel smart for finding it

"Mystery is the modern marketing budget. People don't want to be sold to. They want to figure something out." Every piece of content Formm makes should reward the viewer for watching it — not pitch them.

"Mystery is the modern marketing budget. People don't want to be sold to. They want to figure something out."
@austinsutor — The New Playbook
Series 1 · Education
"Sports Science Simplified"
Short-form video explaining concepts professional sports scientists use — without selling Formm. Build the belief that sports science matters. The app is the conclusion the viewer reaches themselves.
Example posts
"What is ACWR and why every serious lifter should know their number"
"Your squat form isn't broken. Your hip mobility is. Here's the difference."
"Why elite athletes measure HRV every morning (and what it actually means)"
"The fatigue vs technique diagnosis — how sports scientists tell them apart"
Series 2 · Form checks
"The Form Check Files"
Real people's form checks shared publicly (with consent). The before/after of AI analysis. The score, the faults, the fix. Creates massive curiosity — "what would Formm say about my form?"
Formats
Reddit-style anonymous submission → Formm AI analysis → reveal
Gym influencer submits their form check live on camera
"We analysed 100 deadlifts in Delhi gyms. Here's what we found."
Weekly "Form check of the week" community series
Series 3 · Behind the AI
"How Formm Sees Your Body"
Demystify the AI. Show what the model actually detects — joint angles, asymmetry, movement paths. This is the content that converts the cynical. "Oh, it actually does something real."
Example posts
"Formm detected a 7° knee valgus at 60% of squat depth. Here's what that means."
"We showed Formm 3 squats — beginner, intermediate, elite. Here's the difference it sees."
"The 5 movement faults our AI catches that your coach misses"
"How do you score a deadlift? The biomechanics behind the number."
Series 4 · Data stories
"What the Data Says"
Aggregate insights from Formm's user base. This is content nobody else can make. It builds authority, generates press, and makes the audience feel like they're inside something real.
Example posts
"We analysed 10,000 squats. The most common fault: early heel rise at 67%."
"Average Scout Score by training age: <1yr = 51, 1–3yr = 64, 3yr+ = 74"
"Delhi vs Mumbai gym form quality — the data is surprising"
"Users who do 2+ form checks per week improve 2.4x faster than once-a-month users"
⚡
The signal rule: Every piece of content should be valuable even if the viewer never downloads Formm. If it only makes sense after download, it's an ad. If it's genuinely useful whether or not they download, it builds the world. Useful content creates belonging. Belonging creates conversion.
Platform strategy

Form check reveals. Sports science one-liners. Scout Score milestones. Aesthetic movement footage. Primary brand presence. 4–5 posts/week.
🎵 YouTube Shorts / TikTok
"Sports Science Simplified" explainers. Form check files. Behind-the-AI content. 30–90 second formats. Discovery engine — new audiences who don't know Formm yet.
💼 LinkedIn
Founder voice. Build-in-public content. Sports science research breakdowns. Targets NCR's corporate fitness demographic and potential institutional partners. 2–3 posts/week.
Pillar 3 — System

System: The infrastructure that compounds without paid spend

The system is what makes the world self-sustaining. Distribution channels, community loops, institutional partnerships, and referral mechanics that grow without paying for each new user.

🏋️
The Gym Partner Network — NCR's B2B distribution channel
Every premium gym in Gurgaon, Noida, and South Delhi is a potential distribution partner. Not an ad placement — a genuine operational partnership that makes their coaches better.
SYSTEMHIGH ROI
1
Offer coaches free Formm Pro accounts. When a coach uses Formm to analyse a client's form, they become brand advocates. The client sees the coach use it and downloads it themselves. One coach with 20 clients = 20 potential users. Target 50 coaches in NCR = 1,000 warm leads.
2
Create "Formm Certified Gym" status. Gyms that have 10+ active Formm users get a window sticker, social badge, and co-marketing. This creates social proof, gives the gym a differentiator, and makes Formm feel established. Lean on the @realeactives case study — they did $5M on launch day because the world existed before the product.
3
Target premium gyms first: Gold's Gym (Gurugram), Anytime Fitness (multiple NCR), Cult.fit premium locations, boutique strength studios. These attract the target demographic — urban, 25–38, fitness-serious, WHOOP/Garmin users.
🎓
College Sports + Physio Institutional Channel
University sports teams and physiotherapy clinics are high-credibility, low-cost distribution. One institution = dozens of engaged users who talk about it.
INSTITUTIONALCREDIBILITY
1
Partner with 3–5 Delhi/NCR university athletic departments (DU, JNU, Shiv Nadar, Ashoka). Offer free team accounts. The sport science angle is compelling for coaches — they get data they currently can't access. The athletes become organic advocates.
2
Sports physiotherapy clinics are an underrated channel. Physios often see patients for injury rehab who are gym-goers. When a physio recommends Formm as part of return-to-play protocol, the credibility transfer is enormous. Target 10–15 NCR sports physios with a clinical partnership offer.
3
The eventual press story: "The sports science platform used by [University] cricket team is now available for everyone." This is the @lilbieber / @skylrk dynamic from the post — an existing world (elite sports science) with a 9x multiplier applied (Formm). Formm beats the record that was 11 months old.
🔄
The Referral Loop — better data with more users
Unlike most referral programmes, Formm has a genuine product reason for referrals: aggregate movement data improves the AI for everyone. That's the referral pitch — not discounts.
VIRAL LOOPPRODUCT-LED
1
Referral framing: "Invite a training partner and your AI gets smarter. Every form check from your gym improves the model for everyone in your training environment." This is a product-truth referral, not a discount referral. It reinforces the world rather than cheapening it.
2
The Scout Score leaderboard within gym communities. When a user's gym has 5+ Formm users, unlock a gym leaderboard. This creates internal competition, retention, and word-of-mouth simultaneously. The gym becomes the community, Formm becomes the infrastructure.
3
Shareable form check results. Every form analysis generates a shareable card with the score, key metric, and "powered by Formm AI." This is the organic content loop — users share their results, followers see the world, downloads follow. Make sharing the default, not an afterthought.

Launch campaigns — the world arrives before the product

Three campaigns, each building the world further. Campaign 1 runs before launch. Campaign 2 is the launch. Campaign 3 compounds the world after it.

Campaign 1 · Pre-launch · Month 1–2
"What Is Your Movement IQ?"
Build the world. Create the category. Launch nothing yet. The goal is to make "movement quality" a term people in NCR fitness circles use before Formm is in the app store.
What we do
Launch the "Movement IQ" concept — a 30-second selfie video assessment that gives a rough score, no app required. Capture emails.
Founder posts daily about sports science on LinkedIn/Instagram. No product pitches. Pure education.
Send form check kits to 10 NCR gym coaches — free analysis of their signature movement. Get their public reaction on camera.
Partner with 2–3 fitness podcasts to discuss "why form quality is the most undertracked variable in Indian fitness." Don't mention Formm by name — let listeners connect the dots.
Success metrics

The launch is the formality. The world is already built. The campaign reveals the product to people who are already inside the world — not strangers who need convincing.

5 micro-influencers (10K–100K fitness followers, NCR-based) do live form checks on Stories. Real reactions, real scores. Not scripted.

"The Formm 100"
Turn top users into the brand. Find Delhi's 100 most dedicated Formm athletes, make them visible, make others want to join the list.
What we do
Quarterly "Formm 100" — Delhi's top athletes by Scout Score progression. Not highest score: most improved. This is accessible to new users.
Each Formm 100 member gets a profile feature: video, form check highlight, Scout Score journey, what movement changed for them.
Formm 100 members become the brand's human proof — real people, real numbers, real stories. Not influencers. Actual users who happened to be consistent.
Expand to Mumbai and Bangalore gym communities with localised Formm 100 lists.
