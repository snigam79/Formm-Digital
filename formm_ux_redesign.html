01 — STRATEGIC DIRECTION

One product. One daily ritual. One clear identity.

The redesign collapses 8 disconnected apps into a single coherent sports science platform. Everything flows from a central question: what should I do today, and why?

Current — Problems
  • 8 navigation items with equal visual weight
  • Dashboard opens to a feature catalogue
  • Every metric shows 0 on first open
  • No daily ritual — nothing anchors return visits
  • Profile completion nag as the first experience
  • Style Guide & Workshops dilute sports science brand
  • Bespoke Coaching is a generic chatbot
  • Scout Score shows "Top 99%" with 0 data
  • Empty state charts communicate failure
  • Water intake taking prime dashboard real estate
Redesign — Solutions
  • 3 top-level tabs: Train · Recover · Progress
  • Dashboard opens to today's readiness + recommendation
  • Empty states show previews, not zeros
  • Morning check-in = the daily ritual anchor
  • Onboarding earns the first value moment in <3 min
  • Style Guide → removed. Workshops → Resources section
  • Coach opens with user's actual data as context
  • Scout Score locked until 5 form analyses completed
  • Empty states are invitations, not reflections of failure
  • Water intake → Nutrition section only
⚡
The single design question for every decision: Would a tired athlete at 9pm after a hard session actually do this? If the answer is uncertain, simplify until it is.

03 — DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Six rules that govern every screen.

01
One daily ritual anchors everything
The morning readiness check-in is Formm's equivalent of Strava's activity log. It's the one thing we expect users to do every day. Every other feature supports it or flows from it.
02
Empty states are invitations, not failures
No screen ever shows a zero. Instead: "Your squat trend starts here" with a ghost preview of what the populated chart looks like. Progress is always being built.
03
Every number has a plain-language translation
An ACWR of 1.42 and a readiness score of 7.3 mean nothing without context. Every metric shows a status (good/caution/high risk) and one actionable implication.
04
Form Analysis is the hero, not one of seven equals
Form Check gets the primary CTA on the dashboard for new users. The AI video analysis is the product's differentiated core. The visual hierarchy must make this unmistakeable.
05
Bespoke means personalised, not just AI
The coaching chat opens with the user's actual data — their readiness score, last form analysis, and ACWR — as context. "Based on your data this week..." is bespoke. A blank prompt box is not.
06
The platform gets smarter the longer you use it
Every screen shows a confidence indicator when data is sparse and gets richer as data accumulates. This communicates long-term value and is the primary retention mechanism.

Replace blank opening with a data-context card
The chat must open with a "Your data this week" card showing readiness, ACWR, last form check score, and Scout Score. This tells the user immediately that the coach has context about them — not just generic fitness knowledge.
On chat open: fetch GET /api/user/weekly-context. Pass as system prompt to AI. First coach message references this data directly.
2

Dynamic prompt chips generated from the user's data. If ACWR is amber: "How do I manage my training load this week?" If last form check was 3 days ago: "When should I do my next form check?" If readiness is red: "What should I do instead of training today?"

Name the coach and give it a persona
Replace "Bespoke Coaching" header with a named persona — "Coach Formm" or something more characterful. The coaching relationship has a name, a consistent voice, and a sports science grounding. This is a retention differentiator — users form habits with named entities.
10 — TRAINING PLANS

Keep the Quick Workout feature. It's the best UX in the product.

The mood-based Quick Workout generator is genuinely excellent product thinking. The plans section needs one structural change: connect recommendations to readiness data.

1
Connect the Quick Workout mood selector to readiness
If readiness is Green, pre-select "Energetic" or "Motivated." If readiness is Amber, pre-select "Balanced" or "Recovery." If Orange/Red, pre-select "Recovery" or show a gentle nudge: "Based on your readiness today, a Recovery session is recommended — but you choose." The mood selector should feel like a confirmation of the readiness recommendation, not a separate decision.
2
Remove the "Generic Plans" amber warning banner
The current amber banner on Plans ("Complete your profile to get personalised plans") is the third instance of this profile-completion nag. Remove it. Plans should show the best available content with a subtle note that personalisation improves with profile data — not a warning banner that communicates incompleteness.
Replace with: a small info pill "Plans personalise as you use Formm" — not an amber warning.
3
Add a session load preview to each plan
Each workout plan card should show its approximate session load in AU: "Energetic — 60 min — ~540 AU" vs "Recovery — 30 min — ~180 AU." Users can then cross-reference against their daily readiness target and choose appropriately.
11 — PROGRESS & COMMUNITY

Charts that show growth, not emptiness.

The Progress section has the right structure. The empty states and chart designs are the problems.

1
Replace empty charts with placeholder states that show what's coming
The Weekly Activity chart showing an empty grid and Strength Progress showing a flat line at 0kg across 6 weeks are the lowest-confidence moments in the entire app. Replace with: "Log 3 sessions to see your activity chart" with a ghost version of what it will look like. The flat zero line must be removed — it communicates 6 weeks of failure.
2
Replace session counts with load-based metrics
The stats showing "0/7 days this week" and "0 Total Workouts" measure activity counts, not training quality. Add: Weekly Load (AU), Average Readiness this week, and Form Score trend alongside the counts. These are the metrics that communicate sports science value.
3
Add a form trend chart as the hero of Progress
The most differentiating longitudinal metric Formm has is form score trend by movement. This should be the primary chart on the Progress Overview tab — not weekly activity. "Your squat has improved 14% in 8 weeks" is a Formm-specific insight that no other app can show.

13 — NOTIFICATION SYSTEM

Three notifications. No more.

More than three notification types and users turn them all off. Formm's notification system should be ruthlessly minimal.

NotificationTimingCopyPriority
Morning check-in 7:00am (user-configurable) "Good morning, Samrat. Tap to check your readiness." Critical
Post-session RPE T+30 min after session end "How did that feel? Rate your session → " Critical
ACWR alert (amber/red) Triggered on threshold cross Amber: "Your training load is high this week." Red: "Your load needs attention — tap to see why." High
Weekly insight (opt-in) Monday 8:00am "Your Move Better weekly summary is ready." Optional
🚫
Remove immediately: XP and level-up notifications, streak reminder notifications, marketing notifications ("New plans available"), and any notification that fires more than once per day. These train users to ignore all notifications including the critical ones.
